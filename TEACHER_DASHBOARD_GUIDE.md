# TEACHER DASHBOARD GUIDE
**St Francis of Assisi School Portal**

---

## Overview

The Teacher Dashboard is your central hub for managing your classes, monitoring student work, and staying updated with school activities. When you log in as a teacher, you'll see several key sections that help you track and manage your teaching responsibilities.

---

## 1. MY CLASSES

**What it shows:**
- All classes assigned to you as a teacher
- The grade level for each class
- Number of students in each class
- Special designation if you are the Class Teacher

**Example:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 📚 Grade 2 - A                      │
│    Grade 2                          │
│    [Class Teacher]                  │
│                            15       │
│                         Students    │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**What this means:**
- You can quickly see all your teaching assignments
- Class Teachers have additional responsibilities for their assigned class
- The student count helps you know the size of each class

---

## 2. GRADING OVERVIEW

This section provides a complete summary of all homework submissions that need your attention. It's divided into 4 key metrics:

### a) Total Submissions
**What it shows:** The total number of homework assignments submitted by students across all your classes

**Example:** 45 Total Submissions
- This means students have submitted 45 homework assignments in total
- Includes both graded and ungraded submissions
- Shows your overall workload

### b) Pending Grading
**What it shows:** Homework submissions waiting to be graded by you

**Example:** 12 Pending Grading
- These are completed assignments that students have submitted
- They are waiting for you to review and assign marks
- **ACTION REQUIRED:** These need your immediate attention

**What you should do:**
- Click on "Grade pending submissions →" to start grading
- Review each submission and provide marks
- Give feedback to help students improve

### c) Graded
**What it shows:** Homework submissions you have already graded and marked

**Example:** 30 Graded
- These assignments have been reviewed and given marks
- Students can now see their results and feedback
- Shows your progress in completing grading tasks

### d) Late Submissions
**What it shows:** Homework submitted after the due date

**Example:** 3 Late Submissions
- Students submitted these assignments past the deadline
- You can still grade them, but they're marked as late
- Helps you track punctuality and student time management

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: What's the difference between "Total Submissions" and "Pending Grading"?**
A: Total Submissions includes ALL submissions (graded and ungraded). Pending Grading only shows the ones you haven't graded yet.

**Q: Do I need to do something about Late Submissions?**
A: Yes, you should still grade them. The system marks them as late automatically, but they still need your review and marks.

**Q: How do I grade pending submissions?**
A: Click the "Grade pending submissions →" link in the Grading Overview section, or click "Grade Submissions" in the top action buttons.

**Q: Can I see which students haven't submitted homework?**
A: Yes, click on the specific homework in the "Active Homework" section to see submission status for all students.

**Q: Why is the number in "Graded" lower than "Total Submissions"?**
A: This is normal. "Graded" only counts submissions you've already marked. The difference between Total and Graded equals your "Pending Grading" count (plus any that might be in draft status).

## TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE USE

1. **Check Daily:** Review your dashboard each day to stay on top of submissions
2. **Grade Promptly:** Try to keep "Pending Grading" as low as possible
3. **Monitor Late Submissions:** Follow up with students who consistently submit late
4. **Use Quick Actions:** The top buttons save you time navigating the portal
5. **Track Trends:** If you notice many students submitting late, consider if the homework load is appropriate

---

## MATHEMATICS BEHIND THE NUMBERS

**Understanding the relationship:**

```
Total Submissions = Graded + Pending Grading + (Other statuses)

Example:
45 Total Submissions = 30 Graded + 12 Pending Grading + 3 Other
```

**Late Submissions:**
- Can be counted in any of the above categories
- A late submission might be "Pending Grading" or already "Graded"
- The number shows how many late submissions exist across all statuses
